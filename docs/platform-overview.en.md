## Story: Preserving IoT and Data Sovereignty with SSI

Network-connected embedded devices now span from consumer products such as smartwatches and smart locks to high-value systems in industrial and mobility domains.  
In high-end domains, trained professionals can usually enforce full lifecycle management, including safety, security, and fault handling. In consumer IoT, however, users often operate devices without clear control over terms, updates, and downstream data usage.

As a result, even when data is generated by users' own devices, users are often unable to control where that data goes, who uses it, and under what conditions it can be reused.  
IW3IP (IoTxWeb3 Intelligence Platform) addresses this gap by placing **sovereignty of IoT devices and their data** at the center.

IW3IP combines blockchain and smart contracts with **Self-Sovereign Identity (SSI)**.  
This enables decentralized verification of users, devices, and service stakeholders without relying on a single central authority, while automatically enforcing data-sharing permissions as transparent, auditable contracts.  
In short, users define who can use which data and under what conditions, and the platform continuously enforces that agreement.

## Key Points

- IoT growth has expanded user-side responsibility for lifecycle and data governance.
- Consumer IoT still lacks transparency and user control over data usage.
- SSI + smart contracts provide decentralized authentication, authorization, and auditability.
- IW3IP starts with **Phase 1: Data Exchange**, then expands to shared detection outcomes, AI-based decisions, and coordinated control.
